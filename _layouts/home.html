Hi, my name is Xingyu (Alfred) Liu, a fourth-year Ph.D. candidate in Marom's Group at Carnegie Mellon University.

My research focuses on searching for singlet fission materials for the application of organic photovoltaic devices. My work is conducted with both machine learning methods and high-throughput calculations for hierarchical screening of large-scale experimental databases. I designed and developed automated workflow incorporating multi-level model training and materials sampling for generalized candidate search.

Before joining CMU, I was a research assistant at State Key Laboratory of Advanced Technology for Materials Synthesis and Processing at Wuhan University of Technology, where I was studying carbon-based mesoscopic perovskite solar cells advised by Dr. Zhiliang Ku, and studying Cobalt-based pseudocapacitors advised by Dr. Liqiang Mai.

I was a summer intern in the Accelerated Materials Design & Discovery (AMDD) team at Toyota Research Institute from June to August 2021. I worked with Dr. Santosh Suram and Dr. Muratahan Aykol on understanding the effects on active learning agents through specifying learning range on high-throughput experimentation catalysts database.
